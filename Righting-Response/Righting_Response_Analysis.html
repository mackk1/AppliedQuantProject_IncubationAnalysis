Preliminary Analysis

This is preliminary/exploratory analysis of sublethal righting responses of P. armatus. I ran an ANCOVA and on the main effects: site, sex, and block with crab size (CW) as a covariate. A significant block effect was detected. This prelimary analysis indicates that the covariate CW was the strongest effect, however CW is closely linked to crab sex, with males generally being larger than females. There were no significant interactions. This analysis informed the analysis of main effects conducted next.

KM_Righting <- read.csv("~/Applied Quant Project/Righting-Response/Trial1_sublethal.csv")
KM_Righting$Block=as.factor(KM_Righting$Block)
library(survival)

Analysis of Main Effects

This is analysis of righting responses of P. armatus. I tested the main effects: site, sex, block and crab size (CW) by generating Kaplan-Meier curves for righting response data, main effects were tested with a Log Rank test. I next fitted these curves to a Cox proportional hazards model to determine differences in hazard risk between main effects. No significant differences were found between sites, but were detected between sexes and sizes. This helped me determine how to present differences between my main effects.

#KM Curves of main effects

Analysis of Spatial Scale
